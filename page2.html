My Interests in Finance and Data

One of my main academic interests is financial markets and how data can be used to better understand them. Financial markets are influenced by a wide range of factors including economic growth, interest rates, government policy, and investor sentiment. Understanding these forces requires both analytical thinking and the ability to interpret large amounts of information. As I have continued studying finance, I have become increasingly interested in how analysts evaluate companies, industries, and macroeconomic trends when making investment decisions.

Another area that interests me is data visualization. Data visualization helps transform raw numerical data into visual insights that are easier to understand and communicate. Charts, graphs, and dashboards allow analysts to quickly identify patterns, relationships, and trends within complex datasets. Tools like Python, data visualization libraries, and interactive dashboards make it possible to present financial data in ways that are both intuitive and informative.

Combining finance with programming and data science is especially exciting because it allows analysts to explore markets using both quantitative and analytical approaches. By working with financial data, building visualizations, and studying economic indicators, I hope to continue developing technical skills that can help me better understand financial systems and investment strategies.

Topics I Enjoy Learning About

  • Macroeconomic trends
  • Financial modeling
  • Data visualization
